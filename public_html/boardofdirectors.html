Board of Directors

Below you'll find profiles of our executive team here at Habitat for Humanity London.

Kathy Fox (Treasurer)

Kathy has an extensive and diverse background in the financial services sector with the Toronto Dominion Bank Financial Group and brings recognized expertise in project management, risk assessment, operational analysis, audit principles and business development. Her latest role with TD was as a Manager in the Commercial Banking group where she successfully led a group of senior bankers to expand TD's market presence in the commercial business sector.

Kathy also has demonstrated an ongoing commitment to the not-for-profit sector and is an active volunteer with several organizations and has many years of direct community service, including previous board experience.

David Leckie (Build Committee Chair)

David has been a Director on the Habitat for Humanity London Board of Directors since 2006 and presently chairs the Build Committee. He is a professional civil engineer (P.Eng) currently holding the position of Director of Roads & Transportation for the City of London, with whom he has worked since September of 1999.

David is a 1970 graduate of the University of Waterloo, where he earned his Bachelor of Applied Science (Civil) degree. His career has been dominated by over 28 years with the Ministry of Transportation, Ontario, gaining progressive experience in all facets of highway planning, design, construction, materials, maintenance, as well as provincial legislation and policy development. He has held positions with MTO across Ontario, including Toronto, Kingston, North Bay, Owen Sound, New Liskeard, and London.

David is also involved professionally with the Ontario Good Roads Association Board of Directors, with whom he has served since 2002 and reached the position of President in 2008/09.

On the personal side, Dave is married with two grown sons and perseveres as a never-say-die athlete, of questionable skill, in just about every sport ever devised.

Catherine McInerney

Catherine's educational background includes a B.A from the University of Toronto, B.Ed from the University of Western Ontario, and a M.L.I.S. from the University of Western Ontario. Catherine was a 2007 Sponsored Employee for the United Way, and has been Habitat Board secretary since 2006. After working in a variety of library positions, Catherine is presently on leave of absence from her job at the University of Western Ontario and is enrolled in the two year masters program in Industrial Relations and Human Resources at the University of Toronto.

Linda Armstrong (Family Selection & Support Committee Chair)

Linda Armstrong was born and raised on a farm near Paris, Ontario. She graduated in 1977 from The University of Waterloo with a degree in Psychology. After 30 years in business, she is now retired and travels extensively with her husband Paul to feed their keen interest in nature, the environment, and the amazing variety of cultures in the world. Linda is also an avid golfer and gardener, continuing to learn and enjoy both.

Linda Armstrong would say that her personality was most shaped by life growing up on the farm. Her love of nature, work ethic, family values, and a roll-up-the-sleeves, pragmatic style, all were founded during those early days. Linda also credits her childhood experiences with teaching her to look for ways to do things more efficiently, thus her interest in driving change and process improvement throughout her business career.

Linda Armstrong was an influential senior leader in the telecommunications world for almost 30 years. She started with Bell as a part-time clerk while in her final year at University. She credits the coaching and encouragement of some terrific bosses, and the opportunity to progress through a wide variety of functions with keeping her motivated and engaged for more than 20 years. She particularly enjoyed working with teams in implementing many major projects and driving strategic change. After Bell, she led the Canadian Operations at Expertech, a national engineering and construction company, and then took on the role of Vice President National Network Assurance, working with a terrific team at Telus. Most recently, she worked with Ingram Micro Canada, the largest global distributor of consumer and business electronics, to develop logistics and warehousing solutions for a new target market.

A diagnosis of breast cancer in late 2005 became a turning point in her life. Linda decided to leave corporate life, and looked to the community for a way to give back. She was inspired by the work of Habitat for Humanity - finding a good fit with the pragmatic, action-oriented approach to solving a need, and the team-oriented model of an entire community helping a deserving family accomplish their goals. For the past 2 years she's been working with the Family Selection and Support committee with a focus on bringing process efficiencies and supporting families' success with meaningful education programs.

Luke Atkins

Luke is currently a self-employed entrepreneur. He received his BA in Economics and BSc in Computer Science at the University of Western Ontario. Over many years he has developed experience in the banking, health care, and utilities sectors. He also has extensive background in IT project management and web software architectures.

Ethan Ling

As a fourth generation Londoner, Ethan left the city for education and work, only to return to London approximately ten years later hoping to make a difference in his hometown. With stops at Carleton and Ryerson Universities, as well as two years spent in British Columbia, Ethan brings a lot of different experiences to the table. Ethan worked as a Project Manager for a prestigious Custom Homebuilder in Toronto for three years, as a Development Planner in the private sector for three years and as a Municipal Planner with the City of London for three years. Ethan also has experience working in finance at the University of Western Ontario and as an outreach coordinator with a local environmental stewardship group Friends of the Coves. Ethan is new to the Habitat for Humanity London Board of Directors in 2009, but has been volunteering with the organization for several years. He looks forward to serving the community and furthering the vision of Habitat for Humanity through his new role on the board.

Paul Broadhead

Paul has lived in London and the immediate community for over 50 years. He was educated at the University of Western Ontario, and belongs to a number of financial and accounting related professions. Together, he and his wife Heather have four children. Paul and Heather own and operate Broadhead Financial Group Inc. They provide personal and corporate financial consulting to high net-worth business owners and professional clients.

Recently, Paul has looked to the community for opportunities to serve and contribute back. Habitat for Humanity London is one of a number of organizations chosen to support through board, committee and advisory appointments.

His passion? "I love to get up every morning and go for a run, it must be the solitude... that explains why I don't like breakfast meetings."

Phillipe Morin

Phillipe was appointed to the Habitat for Humanity London Board of Directors in January, 2009. He is also a member of both the Marketing/PR and ReStore task force committees.

Phillipe is currently Vice-President, Sales & Marketing for Microtronix Systems Ltd. and has held a wide variety of operational, business development and marketing roles both locally and internationally. His business experience ranges from Telecommunications to Aerospace Industries.

Mr. Morin's community involvement includes working with the mentally handicapped and working with such service organizations as: The Rotary Club of London, Ontario, Junior Achievement and now brings his breadth of leadership, marketing and PR knowledge to Habitat for Humanity, London. Mr. Morin has an MBA from the University of Windsor's MBA for Managers and Professionals programme, and a BA from the University of Western Ontario.

W. Scott Gray

After a lengthy career in both operational and human resources management professional within the provincial and municipal governments, Scott undertook a variety of consulting contracts in both the public and private sector. Since 2001 he has conducted both private and court-ordered mediations as a Roster Mediator with the Ontario Mandatory Mediation Program. Much of his career has been focused on the resolution of workplace conflict. He has also acted in the capacity of chief spokesperson at collective bargaining, the presentation of cases at arbitration and as a management nominee to boards of arbitration. In addition to his degree, a certificate in fine art and a CHRP designation he is also a graduate of the University of Western Ontario's Alternative Dispute Resolution Program and is an active member of both the London Area Mediator's Association and the ADR Institute of Ontario. He has been an active volunteer and Board member with Habitat for Humanity since 2004.
